### Question

An object is completely immersed in water.

Upthrust acts on the object.

Which calculation will correctly give the magnitude of the upthrust?

### Options

- (A) density of the object multiplied by g
- (B) density of the water multiplied by g
- (C) mass of water displaced multiplied by g
- (D) volume of water multiplied by g

### Answer

C

### Explanation

##### Identify
- object is "completely immersed" so the volume of displaced water is equal to the volume of the the object
- **Archimedes' Principle": upward buoyant force (upthust) that is exerted on a body immersed in a fluid, whether fully or partially, is equal to the weight of the fluid that the body displaces
- **Target:** magnitude of upthurst

##### Set-up
Free-body diagram:

Upthrust (from Archimedes' principle):
$$
F=\rho_\text{water} g V_\text{displaced water}
$$

Weight:
$$
W=mg
$$

##### Execute
Since the block is floating, it is in equilibrium:
$$
U = \rho_\text{water} V_\text{displaced water} g
$$

m_\text{water} = \rho_text{water} V_\text{displaced water}

Therefore:

U = mass_\text{water} \times g

### Mark scheme

**Answer: A**
