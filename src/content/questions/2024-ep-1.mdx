Kilowatt-hour ($\text{kWh}$) means: $1000 \text{W} \times 1 \text{hour}$

Watts are joules per second: $1 \text{ W} = 1 \frac{\text{J}}{\text{s}}$

A joule in SI units is given by:
$1 \text{ J} = 1 \text{ kg }\text{m}^{2}\text{ s}^{-3}$

Converting 1 hour into seconds: $1 \text{ hour} = 3600 \text{ s}$

Combining these: $1 \text{ kWh} = 1000 \times (\text{ kg }\text{m}^{2}\text{ s}^{-3}) \times (3600 \text{ s})$
Gives: $\text{ kg }\text{m}^{2}\text{ s}^{-2}$
