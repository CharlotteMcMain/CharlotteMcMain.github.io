### Question

A block of wood is floating in calm water.  
The density of the wood is $700\ \text{kg m}^{-3}$.  
The density of water is $1000\ \text{kg m}^{-3}$.

What percentage of the volume of the block is **above** the waterline?

### Options

- (A) 30%
- (B) 50%
- (C) 70%
- (D) 89%

### Answer

A

### Explanation

##### Identify
- density of wood, $\rho_\text{wood}=700\ \text{kg m}^{-3}$
- density of water, $\rho_\text{water}=1000\ \text{kg m}^{-3}$
- *“…block of wood is **floating** in calm water”* → equilibrium (Archimedes’ principle)
- **Target:** percentage of block above the waterline

##### Set-up
Upthrust:
$$
F=\rho g V
$$

Weight:
$$
W=mg
$$

##### Execute
Since the block is floating, it is in equilibrium:
$$
\rho_\text{water} V_\text{submerged} g
=
\rho_\text{block} V_\text{block} g
$$

Therefore,
$$
\frac{V_\text{submerged}}{V_\text{block}}
=
\frac{\rho_\text{block}}{\rho_\text{water}}
=
\frac{700}{1000}
=
0.7
$$

This means $70\%$ of the block is submerged, so:
$$
1 - 0.7 = 0.3
$$

Hence, **30%** of the block is above the waterline.

### Mark scheme

**Answer: A**

**Examiner’s comments:**  
For the block to float, its weight $(700 \times V \times g)$ must equal the weight of the
water displaced $(1000 \times k \times V \times g)$, where $k$ is the fraction of the block
under water. Equating gives $k = 0.7$, so the fraction above the waterline is $0.3$.
