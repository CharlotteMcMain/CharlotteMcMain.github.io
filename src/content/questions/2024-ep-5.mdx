  The positive zero error means that when it <i>should</i> read 0, it reads $+0.07\text{mm}$.

  Therefore, to get the true diameter, we need to subtract the zero error:

  $$ \text{true diameter} = 2.88-0.07=2.81\ \text{mm}$$

  However, we are asked to give the cross-sectional area of the wire, so we now need to calculate this:

  $$ \text{radius}, r=\frac{d}{2}=\frac{2.81\times10^{-3}}{2}=1.405\times10^{-3}\text{m}$$
  $$ A = \pi\times r^2=\pi \times (1.405\times10^{-3})^2=6.20\times10^{-6}\text{m}^2$$
